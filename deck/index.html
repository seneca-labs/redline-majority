Where we left off

The problems.

PRC submission decks take days to annotate, box by box.

Every round of feedback means manual updates, page by page.

Twenty added pages renumber everything; every reference fixed by hand.

Veeva responses rebuilt from memory, comment by tiresome comment.

The same flags keep coming back: phrasing, commas, decimals.

What's shaping our direction

What you asked for.

A checklist of every comment and its answer, ready to copy-paste into Veeva.

Annotations drafted for the team, not by designers who don't know where the QR codes go.

A way to change a word inside a board without touching the file again.

An AI that reviews the way PRC does and pushes back before you submit.

A tool that lives inside Google, where the team already works.

Two versions, a reviewer and a doer, with real numbers.

The problem

Every submission is built, annotated, and fixed by hand.

150 comments come back. Someone retypes every one.

One missed callout costs another round and another week.

What Redline is

Redline automates the PRC process end-to-end.

It builds and annotates the submission, predicts the review, and preps the Veeva paperwork. Then it takes the feedback, makes the changes in the deck and the assets, verifies nothing was missed, and learns your reviewers every round.
