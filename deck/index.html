Where we left off

In June we walked your Freddie kiosk submission and showed the first version of this tool on it.

The room asked for two versions, a reviewer and a doer, and a proposal with real numbers.

Since then your team's workflow notes arrived, and we built.

What's shaping our direction

Your team's words. The pains from the July email: annotation time, PRC training gaps, knowledge in the wrong heads.

Your cadence. Tuesday submissions, Thursday reviews, around 150 comments a round.

Your stack. Google end to end, designers in their own tools.

The problem

Every submission is built, annotated, and fixed by hand.

150 comments come back. Someone retypes every one.

One missed callout costs another round and another week.

What Redline is

Redline runs the entire PRC round.

It builds and annotates the submission, predicts the review, and preps the Veeva paperwork. Then it takes the feedback, makes the changes in the deck and the assets, verifies nothing was missed, and learns your reviewers every round.
