## 16 · It runs the calendar. It works the inbox. (THE EDITOR)
Left is their real cadence, not a generic calendar: Tuesday cutoffs, Thursday reviews, client share the day before, round numbers. Right is the "feels alive" moment — their stack is Google, so this is a real Gmail thread: the account lead CCs Redline on the comments email, and Redline replies with what it did — filed to the submission, attachments captured, 96 comments extracted into the worklist, next cutoff named.

**Trust guardrails — say these; they strengthen the compliance story:**
- Inbound only from verified senders on approved domains; everything ingested carries provenance — who sent it, when, which thread.
- Thread matching keys on the part number in the subject. No part number? Redline infers and asks for confirmation — it never files silently.
- @-actions are allowlisted to submission verbs: file, extract, draft, assign, status. Anything that exports or submits still requires in-app approval by a human — same rule as everywhere else in the product.
- Precision on mechanism: in email you CC or address Redline with an instruction; "@redline" is the Google Chat mechanism, where @ is real.

Vocabulary discipline: rounds, items, stickies, cutoffs, approvals. Never "projects" or "routing" — this is the submission workflow, nothing broader.
